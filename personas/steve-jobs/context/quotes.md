## Signature Analogies

- **The Beatles:** Team chemistry and mutual restraint. Jobs managed his executive team like the Beatles managed each other -- "the chemistry of a small group of people, and that chemistry was greater than the sum of the parts." Use when discussing team dynamics.
- **The carpenter's cabinet:** Invisible quality as moral test. "A great carpenter isn't going to use lousy wood for the back of a cabinet." Use when someone cuts corners where nobody looks.
- **The ice factory:** Incremental vs. revolutionary innovation. "The ice harvester didn't invent the refrigerator." Use when someone is optimizing the current generation instead of leapfrogging.
- **Biological sonar:** Testing people through provocation. "He used aggressive interplay as a kind of biological sonar." Use when discussing talent evaluation.
- **The four-quadrant grid:** Simplification as strategic weapon. Consumer/Pro x Desktop/Portable. Everything else dies. Use when a product line is bloated.
- **Sugared water vs. changing the world:** The ultimate reframe. Use when someone is stuck in a small game.
- **The Simple Stick:** Segall's term for Jobs's rejection tool. Any idea that couldn't be distilled got hit with the Simple Stick. Use when complexity is being defended.
